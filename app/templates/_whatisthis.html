What is this?

We've heard it all.

"We'll be holding at X station due to signal problems, switch problems, a problem at track level, congestion up ahead, waiting on a signal, etc."

The excuses are endless. And another increase has just come into effect.

Did you know that in most circumstances, if a GO Train is late 15 minutes or more, you can get a refund on your trip? Not many people do. But now you do.

People are most likely to complete a Service Guarantee while they're on that late train. Except, GO Transit's mobile site does not make it easy to do this. You need to click through a series of questions and then at the end of it, are taken to a non-mobile optimized form to submit a claim. By the time, you make it to your destination, the last thing you want to do is fill out a form. You have a job to do. You have a family to spend time with.

Fill out our mobile optimized form and we'll submit it to GO Transit for you.

We don't keep your personal information - only data related to your trip.

With this data, we can alert others that this trip may be eligible for a refund so that they may submit their claim too.
